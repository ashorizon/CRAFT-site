Abstract

Autoregressive traffic simulators trained on ego-centric driving logs can suffer from a local-to-global context mismatch when deployed in globally observable closed-loop environments. This mismatch may cause unrealistic behaviors such as abnormal stops, unsafe interactions, and rule violations.

We propose CRAFT, a Contextual Reference Alignment Framework for Traffic Simulation. CRAFT discovers context-induced failures through what-if rollouts, trains a Contextual Preference Evaluator, and uses it as a plug-in test-time guidance module to improve closed-loop realism, safety, and rule compliance without retraining the base simulator.

Why Local Logs Fail in Closed-Loop Simulation

Logged driving data captures partial local observations, whereas closed-loop simulation exposes the complete scene context. This mismatch can lead to unrealistic interactions, abnormal stops, and rule violations.

Method Overview

CRAFT learns contextual preferences from simulator-induced failures, then applies them as plug-in guidance during closed-loop rollout.

Training: Learn Contextual Preferences from What-If Rollouts

A frozen simulator generates diverse rollouts, from which CRAFT identifies failures and constructs preference pairs under complete scene context.

Inference: Reweight Candidate Actions with CPE

CPE evaluates Top-K candidate actions using one-step lookahead scenes and reweights the simulator distribution without retraining the base model.

Test-Time Alignment Analysis

CPE identifies emerging abnormal behaviors before failures fully develop and suppresses their accumulation during long-horizon closed-loop rollout.

Robustness Analysis

CRAFT remains effective across both training and inference configurations. More preference data consistently improves performance, while a moderate guidance scale and candidate pool size provide the most stable test-time alignment.

Key Findings

  • Better closed-loop safety: CRAFT reduces collision, off-road, and traffic violation rates.
  • General plug-in guidance: CRAFT improves both CAT-K and SMART without retraining the base simulator.
  • More rational rollouts: CRAFT mitigates abnormal stops and unsafe interactions.
  • Stable test-time alignment: CPE provides proactive risk signals and reduces error accumulation.
