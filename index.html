Abstract

Autoregressive traffic simulators trained on ego-centric driving logs can suffer from a local-to-global context mismatch when deployed in globally observable closed-loop environments. This mismatch may cause unrealistic behaviors such as abnormal stops, unsafe interactions, and rule violations.

We propose CRAFT, a Contextual Reference Alignment Framework for Traffic Simulation. CRAFT discovers context-induced failures through what-if rollouts, trains a Contextual Preference Evaluator, and uses it as a plug-in test-time guidance module to improve closed-loop realism, safety, and rule compliance without retraining the base simulator.

Overview

CRAFT targets the mismatch between partial observations in driving logs and complete context in closed-loop simulation.

Method Overview

CRAFT uses a frozen simulator to generate what-if rollouts, converts discovered failures into preference supervision, and trains CPE to score behaviors under complete scene context.

Training Strategy

  1. What-if rollout: generate diverse closed-loop rollouts from logged initial scenes.
  2. Failure discovery: detect abnormal stops, collisions, off-road events, and traffic violations.
  3. Preference construction: compare better and worse rollouts under the same scene context.
  4. CPE training: learn dense contextual preference scores for candidate behaviors.

Inference Strategy

During inference, CPE evaluates Top-K candidate actions with one-step lookahead and reweights the base simulator probabilities before sampling.

  1. Extract Top-K candidate actions from the base simulator.
  2. Construct lookahead scenes for each candidate.
  3. Use CPE to score candidate behaviors.
  4. Reweight priors with CPE scores and sample the final action.

Guidance: Pposti,t,k ∝ Ppriori,t,k · exp(β · Rt(k))

Robustness Analysis

CRAFT remains effective under different preference data sizes, guidance scales β, and candidate pool sizes K.

Key Findings

  • Better closed-loop safety: CRAFT reduces collision, off-road, and traffic violation rates.
  • General plug-in guidance: CRAFT improves both CAT-K and SMART without retraining the base simulator.
  • More rational rollouts: CRAFT mitigates abnormal stops and unsafe interactions.
  • Stable test-time alignment: CPE provides proactive risk signals and reduces error accumulation.
